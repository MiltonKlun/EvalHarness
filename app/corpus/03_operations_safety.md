# Meridian Robotics — Operations & Safety Policy

## Pre-flight checks
Every inspection flight requires a two-person crew: a pilot and a safety observer. The
pilot completes a 12-point pre-flight checklist before takeoff. If wind speed at the
turbine hub exceeds the drone's rated tolerance, the flight is cancelled — no exceptions.

## No-fly conditions
Meridian drones do not fly in the following conditions: lightning within 10 kilometres,
visibility below 500 metres, or icing conditions on the turbine blades. The safety
observer has unilateral authority to abort any flight.

## Data handling
Inspection footage is uploaded to TurbineSight over an encrypted link within 24 hours of
each flight. Raw footage is retained for seven years to meet client audit requirements.
Personal data is never collected during inspections; the drones are pointed at turbines,
not people.

## Incident history
Meridian has recorded three minor incidents since 2019, all involving hard landings in
gusty conditions. No injuries have ever occurred. The full incident log is confidential
and is not shared outside the safety team.
